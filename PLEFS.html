How do you make a myopic, 40 year old, organically created mainframe software usable for modern humans?

The Why

40 years of business innovation without technology innovation leads to feature bloat. The outdated format that becomes increasingly more difficult to learn & update. And with the limited number of people who knew how to navigate the software retiring, it was time to do something.

Create Alignment

How do we all agree on what a “better product” means?

  • everyone agrees on what’s terrible, but there’s rarely consensus on how to make it better
  • lots of politics and opinions
  • we all need to agree on “better” to prevent it from being worse
  • so how do we protect the future from recreating the past

The Transition

The change was to migrate from a mainframe oriented software to a business oriented one. Nobody should have to understand 1980's mainframe logic to do their job. But everyone should understand their business Orienting the software around the business makes it easier for humans to use because its puts the conceptual model of the software back in the ballpark of their day-to-day job.
